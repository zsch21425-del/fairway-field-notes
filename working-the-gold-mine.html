Chapter One

What mining actually means

You have three gold mines sitting right there at Fairway. Find the customers who are right now in a position to trade, upgrade, or refinance — then reach out with a specific reason to talk.

01

The Sold Database

Every person who ever bought a car from Fairway Subaru. Years of relationships sitting in the CRM, untouched since the thank-you card.

02

The Service Drive

Every person getting their car serviced today, right now. Already on-site, already drinking your coffee, already thinking about their car.

03

Orphan Owners

Customers whose original salesperson left the dealership. No one is calling them. The first rep to re-engage becomes their person.

The idea is simple: not every customer in those pools is ready to move today, but some are — and the CRM can tell you which ones. Your job is to find them and start the conversation before Carvana does.

Chapter Four

What to actually say

Specific and honest beats generic and pushy, every time. Lead with real numbers they can verify.

Sample Equity Outreach

The text that actually gets a reply

Sent to a customer who bought a 2023 Outback 28 months ago:

Hey Sarah, it's Wes at Fairway. Ran a quick check on your 2023 Outback — with where used values are right now, you've got about $4,200 in equity. We've got a 2026 Forester Hybrid coming in next week that would land you close to the same payment. Worth a 10-minute conversation?
— Specific. Honest. Low-pressure. Gets responses.

The anatomy of why this works: it names a specific vehicle, cites a real number, proposes a concrete next step (the incoming Forester), anchors value (same payment), and asks for a small commitment (10 minutes, not a test drive). Every generic "just checking in" text your competitor sends makes this one land harder.

The TCPA Caveat

Don't mass-text old records without documented consent. Stick to customers who opted in at original purchase — most did. Respect opt-outs within 10 business days across every channel. Phone calls and personalized emails are safer than bulk texts. Penalties are $500–$1,500 per violation, and automotive cases have produced settlements in the millions.

At average Subaru gross plus F&I, that's real money — from customers who would have otherwise gotten a generic email and bounced to Carvana.

The reason the math works: these aren't cold leads. These are people you already sold. They know the dealership, they know the service drive, and most of them genuinely liked the experience. You're not prospecting — you're re-engaging. The conversion rate is always higher than new-lead channels, and the acquisition cost is zero.

The other reason it works: almost nobody at your store does it consistently. The reps who hit their monthly unit numbers the first three weeks of the month get complacent. The reps who miss it get desperate and pivot to fresh leads. Neither group is working the database. That is your opening.

Thirty minutes Monday morning. Three to five conversations a day. Twice through the service drive. One honest text at a time. Mining isn't magic — it's the discipline of remembering that the people who already trusted you once are the most likely to trust you again.
